How does the Family Map benefit early childhood programs?

The Family Map provides a systematic way to identify strengths within the home, reveal addressable needs that impact a child’s health and educational outcomes, and screen for potential trauma in the child and family’s life. This interview tool builds a foundation of trust and mutual helpfulness to accelerate the educator-parent partnership working together to benefit the child.

When you know the family’s strengths and potential risk factors by asking the right questions, you can engage the right resources to benefit the child and family. The Family Map Inventory focuses on the family as a whole by looking at 12 areas that we know to be most impactful to child growth and development. The interactive, electronic interview system provides you with the appropriate questions backed by research to reveal key data. The system then provides immediate feedback with individualized child & family reporting which can be used to offer targeted resources with the aid of the integrated goal setting documentation. Agency level reporting on family strengths, adverse childhood experiences and potential trauma exposure are useful for programmatic planning and monitoring.

An effective tool for family engagement

Many of our clients find that the Family Map replaces 2-3 other assessments or processes that they were using to learn a child’s family & home background. The Inventory is thorough, and research proven to deliver valid results that are useful in your family engagement. The interview can be done at enrollment, home visits, and/or parent-teacher conferences. Most programs repeat the interview every 6-9 months. Family strengths and risks are clearly identified based on a parent’s response. You can discuss these strengths and risks at the conclusion. Together, you and the parent can determine whether to set goals to address risks.

Robust reporting allows you to see the family’s progress by comparing two interviews and giving an “at-a-glance” indicator of a family’s strength or risk in key factors. Beyond measuring strengths and needs within a family, the Inventory includes a goal setting template called the Family-Partnership Agreement that allows you to track progress as you continue to work with the child’s primary caregiver. You can also send short, research-based parenting tips along with other resource information that support the parent’s goals directly from the Family Partnership Agreement to the parent by email or text message.
