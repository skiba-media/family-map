    Step-by-step Tour Covering

    • Family engagement training in the use of The Family Map
    • Practice with basic interview techniques
    • Strategies to help strengthen the parent-educator partnership
    • Access to ongoing web-based learning opportunities

    Training Features

    • Web based learning session and in-person training
    • 6-hour live learning session
    • Maximum group size is 25
    • All materials provided
    • practitioner is authorized to conduct the Family Map Inventory
    • Ongoing web-based learning
    Classes are scheduled as needed. They are typically held at Family Map headquarters in Little Rock, Arkansas, but may be held at your site with appropriate expenses for the trainer’s travel covered. We can customize a training and implementation plan for your agency.

    How it works

    We train your staff on the most up-to-date research on risk and protective factors in families.

    Then, we give step-by-step instruction and practice using The Family Map interviews and help you implement interviews. Use them at yearly home visits, at parent-teacher conferences, or at enrollment.
    The result is better family partnership.

    Educators trained in The Family Map

    • Learn about each family’s strengths and needs.
    • Determine the services or resources that will be most helpful to each family.
    • Individualize support for children in the classroom or visitation setting.
    • Have a framework for ongoing assessment and progress documentation.

    Educators say The Family Map

    • Makes parents feel closer to teachers.
    • Cuts down on other agency paperwork.
    • Helps teachers make important changes to their classrooms.
